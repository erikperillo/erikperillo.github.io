About me

I am a brazilian Computer Science/Engineering student who is passionate about the strange and funny things in our Universe. I like creating new stuff and defying anything, even the obvious.

I'm very into robots. More specifically, I'm into making robots smarter, that is, Artificial Intelligence. Though still a padawan, I'm highly fascinated by this still open subject. I have some experience with Computer Vision and Machine Learning.

I'm an ex-member and currently a mentor at Phoenix, a robotics group formed by undergraduate students at my university, Unicamp.

Baleia

Baleia is a robot for the Trekking challenge of Brazilian robotics organization Robocore. In this challenge, an autonomous robot must go through a trajectory composed of different determined locations while avoiding obstacles. All this stuff must be done as fast as possible! In order to complete the task, we use a variety of sensors as well as Computer Vision Techniques. I was the leader of the project, besided helping project and build the robot. We achieved a historical podium in the very first competition Baleia got in. I no longer work on this project.

Baleia in Action

Piranha

Piranha is our new robot for the Trekking challenge. It is faster, smarter and more ambitious. A new mechanical structured combined with an embedded GPU provide the robot way more power. I was also the leader of the project besides being responsible for the computer vision component of the robot. Piranha won the XXI Winter Challenge, as reported by brazilian media! It was three times as fast as the runner-up. We're very proud of our work. I no longer work on this project.
